The Pan Fried Taco

This simple yet amazing dish will have your belly full and your ming content. For all those taco lovers out there, this recipe is a simple must-try!

You can make crispy and savory pan-fried tacos with a quick, seasoned ground beef filling. Warming the corn tortillas beforehand prevents them from cracking as you fold them, and securing them with toothpicks keeps the filling inside during frying.

Ingredients

Filling

  • 1 pound ground beef, at least 85/15
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped white onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 packet taco seasoning, or homemade mix (see instructions below)
  • 1/2 cup water

Assembly and Friying

  • 10 to 12 corn tortillas
  • 1 to 2 cups shredded cheese, like cheddar, Mexican blend, or Monterey Jack
  • About 1/2 cup vegetable oil, plus more as needed

Homemade Seasoning (Optional)

  • 2 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional, for heat)

Instructions

  1. Make the filling:In a large skillet over medium-high heat, brown the ground beef with the chopped onion, breaking it up with a wooden spoon. Drain the excess grease. Add the minced garlic and cook for one more minute until fragrant. Stir in the taco seasoning and water, bring to a simmer, then reduce the heat to low and cook for 5 to 8 minutes until the sauce thickens.
  2. Warm the tortillas:To make the tortillas pliable and prevent them from breaking, wrap a stack of 3 to 4 in a damp paper towel and microwave for about 30 seconds.
  3. Assemble the tacos:Lay the warmed tortillas on a flat surface. Spoon 2 to 3 tablespoons of the beef mixture onto one half of each tortilla, and top with a small sprinkle of shredded cheese.
  4. Heat the oil:Heat about 1/2 inch of oil in a clean skillet or griddle over medium-high heat. The oil is ready when a small piece of tortilla immediately sizzles.
  5. Fry the tacos:Carefully place 2 to 3 tacos into the hot oil at a time. Use tongs to hold them folded for the first 30 seconds to help them hold their shape. Fry for 2 to 3 minutes per side, or until the tortillas are golden brown and crispy.
  6. Drain and serve:Use tongs to remove the tacos from the oil, tilting them to drain any excess oil back into the pan. Place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain, and serve hot with your favorite toppings, such as shredded lettuce, salsa, and sour cream.
